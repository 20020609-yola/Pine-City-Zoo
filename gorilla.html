GORILLA

Gorillas are powerful, intelligent primates native to Africa's forests. They're social animals, living in groups led by dominant males, and communicate through vocalizations, body language, and facial expressions.

Gorillas are herbivores, feeding on leaves, shoots, and fruits. Despite their strength, they're generally gentle, and conservation efforts aim to protect them from habitat loss, poaching, and disease

Observing gorillas in their natural habitat offers a unique glimpse into their complex social behavior and emotional depth.

Where Can You Find Them

Gorillas can be found in the wild in central Africa, specifically in the forests of countries such as Rwanda, Uganda, and the Democratic Republic of Congo.

They inhabit mountainous regions, like the Virunga Mountains, and lowland forests. Some notable places to observe gorillas include Volcanoes National Park in Rwanda, Bwindi Impenetrable National Park in Uganda
