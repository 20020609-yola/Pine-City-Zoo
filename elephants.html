ELEPHANTS

Elephants are large, intelligent, and social mammals characterized by their distinctive trunks, tusks, and large ears. They belong to the family Elephantidae and are the largest land animals on Earth. Elephants are known for their exceptional memory, complex social structures, and empathetic behavior. They inhabit savannas, forests, and grasslands in sub-Saharan Africa and southern Asia, playing a vital role in shaping their ecosystems. With their impressive size, intelligence, and emotional depth, elephants are fascinating creatures that hold significant cultural and ecological importance.
