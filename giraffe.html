GIRAFFE

The giraffe is the tallest land animal on Earth, known for its incredibly long neck and legs

Native to the savannas and open woodlands of Africa, giraffes are gentle herbivores that live in loose social groups.

Their distinctive coat patterns—unique to each individual—provide camouflage in their natural habitat

Despite their height, giraffes have only seven neck vertebrae, just like humans.

They use their long, prehensile tongues, which can be up to 45 cm long, to grasp foliage.

Giraffes are generally quiet animals but can communicate using low-frequency sounds.

Sadly, giraffe populations are declining due to habitat loss and poaching, making conservation efforts crucial for their survival.
